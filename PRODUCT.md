# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

School directors and administrators in Morocco evaluating bus tracking for their school; secondarily transport operators, and parents checking the product's legitimacy. French-first readers (Arabic-adjacent market), often on mobile, WhatsApp-native communication culture.

## Product Purpose

Marketing site for Transco, the school vertical of the Transtra platform (transtra.net). One-page pitch: what the product does (live bus tracking, parent app, school console, QR-verified boarding), why it can be trusted, and a single conversion goal — request a demo.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy, honest, product-real. The site shows the actual product UI as its imagery (real console mock, real phone home screen, real map data from Tétouan) rather than marketing abstractions. Copy is plain French with correct typography (guillemets, espaces insécables); demo data is labeled honestly. Gold = Transco school identity (logo only, never status); teal = Transtra platform trust.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient text, hero metrics, icon-card grids, tracked-uppercase eyebrows.
- Stock photos of smiling children / school buses instead of the real product.
- Overpromising copy ("révolutionnaire", "IA"); the product sells on honesty about data freshness.

## Design Principles

1. **Product as imagery** — every visual is a faithful mock of the real console/app, never decoration.
2. **Honesty converts** — state data freshness, label demo data, never fake urgency.
3. **Ported, not invented** — visual system comes 1:1 from the product (Transtra/web globals.css); the site must feel like the console it sells.
4. **One page, one goal** — every section funnels to "Demander une démo".
5. **Parents are the emotional core** — the school buys, but the parent's peace of mind is the story.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA contrast; `prefers-reduced-motion` honored everywhere (site claims it about the product, so the site must practice it); content visible without JS; French-first with bilingual intent (fr/en in product).
